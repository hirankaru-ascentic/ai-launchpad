Before you apply

Is this for you?

Places are limited and participants are selected through an application process. Apply only if both of these fit.

Who can apply
  • 3rd or 4th year IT undergraduate
  • Recent IT graduate
  • From any university or institution
  • Ready to stop learning and start building
What we ask of you
  • Attend all 6 Saturday sessions
  • Spend real time developing your project
  • Work closely with your assigned mentor
  • Build a solution to a real-world challenge
  • Present at the Showcase if shortlisted

You'll be trained and supported throughout the journey.

Good to know

Questions, answered.

How much time will it take each week?
A 2-hour session every Saturday for 6 of the 8 weeks, plus your own time building your project and a weekly check-in with your mentor. The more you put in, the stronger your final project.
Do I need to be a strong coder?
No. You need to be a 3rd/4th year IT undergrad or recent graduate willing to learn and build. The program is about using AI to create value — your mentor guides you through the rest.
What exactly will I build?
You'll identify a real-world problem worth solving and design, prototype, or build an AI-enabled solution or proof of concept. The idea is yours — the mentorship helps you make it real.
What happens if I'm shortlisted?
Selected participants present their projects at the Showcase Event to an audience of industry professionals and peers. You'll be trained and supported to present with confidence.
When do applications open?
Applications open on 1 July 2026. Until then, explore the program here and add the date to your calendar so you're ready the moment registration goes live.
